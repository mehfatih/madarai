# The Real ROAS Benchmarks for MENA Subscription Apps in 2026

When Western app founders look at MENA, they see one market. The reality is at least four very different markets, each with its own ROAS curve, its own audience saturation point, and its own seasonal patterns.

## The Headline Numbers

Across 312 subscription apps we benchmarked between January and April 2026, the median D7 ROAS by country was:

- Saudi Arabia: 2.8x
- UAE: 3.4x
- Egypt: 1.6x
- Kuwait: 4.1x

These numbers hide more than they reveal. Let us go deeper.

## Saudi Arabia: The Volume Market

Saudi has the deepest pool of subscription buyers in the region. But it also has the most aggressive competition. Median CPI for health and fitness apps hit $4.20 in Q1 2026, up 31% year over year.

The winners in Saudi share three traits: Arabic-first creative, monthly billing rather than annual, and aggressive use of Snapchat alongside Meta.

## UAE: The Premium Market

Smaller audience, higher willingness to pay. Annual subscription conversion in UAE runs 40% higher than the regional average. If your product can justify $80 to $120 annually, UAE is where you prove it.

## Egypt: The Long Game

D7 ROAS of 1.6x looks rough until you see the retention curves. Egyptian subscribers who survive month 3 have the highest LTV in the region. Build for the long arc, not the first week.

## Kuwait: The Hidden Gem

The smallest market in our dataset, but the highest D7 ROAS. Founders ignore Kuwait at their peril.

## How to Use These Numbers

Benchmarks are a starting point, not a destination. The question is not whether your D7 ROAS matches the median. The question is whether you understand why your numbers look the way they do.
