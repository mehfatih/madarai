# Case Study: How One Meditation App Cut Churn by 31% in 90 Days

In December 2025, a four-person meditation app team in Istanbul had a problem. Their monthly churn had crept from 6.2% to 9.4% over two quarters. Their MRR growth had flatlined. The founder was raising a seed round and the numbers were not telling a good story.

By March 2026, their churn was 6.5%. MRR growth was back to 14% month over month. The seed round closed at the top of their target range.

This is what happened.

## The Initial Diagnosis

When the team connected their RevenueCat and AppsFlyer accounts to Madar in early January, the first insight surfaced within 48 hours. It was not what they expected.

The churn problem was not a churn problem. It was an acquisition problem.

Their D30 churn rate by acquisition source revealed a sharp pattern. Users acquired through Meta lookalike audiences churned at 12.8%. Users acquired through Google search churned at 4.1%. The team had been heavily increasing Meta spend for six months. The churn rise was a direct consequence.

## The Reallocation

The team did three things in January:

1. Cut Meta lookalike spend by 60%
2. Tripled Google search spend
3. Built a new onboarding flow specifically for high-intent search users

The reallocation was painful in the short term. New subscribers per month dropped 18% in January. The team held their nerve.

## The Compound Effect

By the end of February, retention had already shifted. The new cohort acquired in January was behaving like the Google cohort, not the Meta cohort. Their D30 retention was 89.2%.

March brought the compound effect. Lower churn meant the cohorts stacked. MRR began climbing again, even though acquisition volume was still down. By the end of March, MRR was 11% higher than in December.

## What They Learned

The team's biggest takeaway was not about Meta or Google. It was about the question they had been asking.

They had been asking: How do we reduce churn?

The right question was: Why are we acquiring users who churn?

A dashboard could show them their churn rate. Only a system that connected acquisition cohorts to retention curves could show them why.

## The Numbers

- D30 churn: 9.4% to 6.5% (31% reduction)
- MRR growth rate: 0% to 14% month over month
- Seed round closed: top of target range
- Time to first Madar insight: 48 hours
